⚠️ Problem Identification
The Accessibility and Vision Impairment Challenge in Malaysia
Visual impairment and myopia are major public health concerns in Malaysia. According to the National Eye Surveys (2024), the prevalence of blindness among adults aged ≥ 50 years is 0.6–1.6 %, with untreated cataracts and diabetic retinopathy as leading causes. The IAPB Vision Atlas reports that sight loss continues to rise due to population ageing and limited access to affordable eye care services. Meanwhile, a systematic review by the Ministry of Health and IMU University (2026) found that up to 42.7 % of Malaysian adolescents experience myopia, with higher rates among females and Chinese Malaysians. 
These conditions severely restrict independence, mobility, and information access. Existing assistive technologies—such as imported smart glasses and handheld readers—are cost prohibitive (RM 10 000–RM 15 000) and not locally serviceable, leaving many visually impaired Malaysians without practical support.
👥 Target Users
•	Primary: Blind and severely visually impaired individuals needing real time obstacle detection and text to speech assistance.
•	Secondary: Myopic and low vision users requiring magnified or audio based feedback for reading and navigation.
🏭 Industry Focus
OPTINAV operates within the assistive technology and healthcare innovation sector, addressing the lack of affordable, AI powered wearable solutions for Malaysia’s visually impaired community. By integrating ESP32 S3 + OV5640 camera, distance sensors, and PAM8403 driven audio output, OPTINAV enables users to:
•	Detect obstacles and objects in real time.
•	Read printed text via OCR and receive speech feedback.
•	Interact with an AI chatbot for contextual understanding of captured scenes.
•	Benefit from modular, locally sourced components for easy maintenance and scalability.
