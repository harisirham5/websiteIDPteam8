Understanding the Accessibility and Vision Impairment Challenge in Malaysia

The Accessibility and Vision Impairment Challenge

Visual impairment and myopia are major public health concerns in Malaysia. According to the National Eye Surveys (2024), the prevalence of blindness among adults aged ≥ 50 years is 0.6–1.6 %, with untreated cataracts and diabetic retinopathy as leading causes. The IAPB Vision Atlas reports that sight loss continues to rise due to population ageing and limited access to affordable eye care services.

Meanwhile, a systematic review by the Ministry of Health and IMU University (2026) found that up to 42.7 % of Malaysian adolescents experience myopia, with higher rates among females and Chinese Malaysians.

These conditions severely restrict independence, mobility, and information access. Existing assistive technologies—such as imported smart glasses and handheld readers—are cost prohibitive (RM 10,000–RM 15,000) and not locally serviceable, leaving many visually impaired Malaysians without practical support.

Target Users & Industry Focus

Primary Users: Blind and severely visually impaired individuals needing real-time obstacle detection and text-to-speech assistance.

Secondary Users: Myopic and low vision users requiring magnified or audio-based feedback for reading and navigation.

Industry Focus: readlens operates within the assistive technology and healthcare innovation sector, addressing the lack of affordable, AI-powered wearable solutions for Malaysia's visually impaired community. By integrating ESP32 S3 + OV5640 camera, distance sensors, and PAM8403 driven audio output, readlens enables users to:

  • Detect obstacles and objects in real time.
  • Read printed text via OCR and receive speech feedback.
  • Interact with an AI chatbot for contextual understanding of captured scenes.
  • Benefit from modular, locally sourced components for easy maintenance and scalability.

Project Goal

readlens is an affordable, wearable AI smart glasses system designed to assist blind and myopic patients in daily navigation and reading tasks. It integrates a camera, distance sensor, and AI-powered smartphone app to perform object identification, obstacle detection, and text recognition, providing real-time audio feedback through embedded speakers.

Solution Strategy:

  • 📷 Utilize ESP32 S3 + OV5640 camera for real-time image capture and distance sensing.
  • 🔊 Implement audio feedback system using PAM8403 amplifier and micro earphone drivers for clear speech output.
  • 🤖 Develop readlens App (AI-powered) to process captured images for OCR or object detection and allow users to query the AI chatbot for contextual understanding.
  • ⚙️ Ensure plug-and-play modular design for easy assembly, maintenance, and local component sourcing in Malaysia.
  • 🧭 Provide real-time obstacle alerts to enhance mobility safety for visually impaired users.
