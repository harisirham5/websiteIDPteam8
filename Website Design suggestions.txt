It directly contradicts your mission of helping blind and myopic individuals. Replace it with "Advanced Assistive Navigation System".Add a High-Contrast CTA Button: Right under your main hook, add a bold, solid-colored button.Example: [Explore Our Prototype] linking directly to the Engineering Design page.Convert the Problem Paragraph into Stat Callouts: Instead of a long block of text about Malaysia's statistics, use huge numbers to grab attention:300,000+ -> Visually impaired citizens in Malaysia.RM 0 -> The target cost accessibility goal for low-income patients.👥 2. Our Team Page (/pageourteam/index.html)Standardize Profile Cards: Ensure every team member has a uniform, high-quality headshot framed in identical circular or rounded-square containers.Role Badges: Instead of plain text for roles (e.g., Project Manager, Lead Hardware Engineer), use colored capsule badges (pills) to make roles instantly scannable.Social/GitHub Icons: Add small, clean, high-contrast SVG icons linking to each member's GitHub or LinkedIn profile underneath their bio.⚠️ 3. Problem Identification (/pageproblemidentification/index.html)Use a Split-Screen Layout: On desktop, split the viewport. Left side: A sticky, high-contrast graphic showing the real-world challenges a blind person faces. Right side: Scrollable text blocks highlighting your data.Bullet the Root Causes: Break down the specific issues (lack of affordable tech, heavy battery packs, slow processing) into an accordion list or clean bullet points rather than dense paragraphs.🛠️ 4. Engineering Design (/pageengineeringdesign/index.html)Implement a Dynamic Process Timeline: Your engineering phases should look like a chronological timeline (Phase 1: Ideation \(\rightarrow \) Phase 2: CAD Modeling \(\rightarrow \) Phase 3: Hardware Integration) using a connected vertical line or step-indicator UI component.Image Captions & Alt Text: Engineering diagrams, circuit schematics, and 3D CAD renders must be accompanied by large, dark text descriptions underneath. Ensure they have clear HTML alt tags so screen readers can describe the design to a visually impaired stakeholder.📋 5.
